Heritage meets automation

A neighborhood favorite, rebuilt as an AI-operated guest-service platform.

Little Kitchen of Westport opened in May 1987 on Main Street with three dine-in tables and grew into a long-standing Fairfield County destination for Chinese, Malaysian, Thai, Indonesian, Taiwanese, sushi, takeout, delivery, and catering. This concept keeps that trust and warmth intact while making AI the primary digital front-of-house employee.

Brand posture

The experience is designed to feel upscale, warm, efficient, and Westport-appropriate: lacquer red, aged gold, warm ivory, black ink, and restrained jade accents, paired with language that sounds like polished hospitality rather than restaurant software.

Operational rules the AI follows

The intelligence works because the system respects restaurant reality and avoids overpromising.

Never confirm a reservation unless the reservation system confirms it.
Never promise an item that is 86’d or unavailable.
Always read back orders before final submission.
Always handle severe allergy questions carefully and note staff confirmation where needed.
Offer alternate times before ending a fully booked reservation request.
Offer alternate dishes when an item is unavailable.
Escalate angry guests, refund disputes, health-related complaints, and sensitive service issues.
Capture intent after hours and give the guest the next best action instead of a dead end.
